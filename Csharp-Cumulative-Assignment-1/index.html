Costumes

Kathak ghungroos are a set of small metallic bells tied around a dancer's ankles, traditionally used in classical Indian dance to emphasize rhythmic footwork and movements. They are integral to the dance, accentuating beats and adding to the performance's auditory appeal.

A ballet dress, often called a tutu, is a lightweight, fitted costume typically made of tulle, designed to allow freedom of movement while showcasing the grace and precision of a ballet dancer's movements. It is iconic for its structured skirt and elegant style.

Ballet shoes are lightweight, flexible footwear designed to support a dancer's feet, providing balance and allowing smooth, graceful movements. They are usually made from soft leather, satin, or canvas, with thin soles for close contact with the floor.

Dancing Through Life: Express your emotions through Dance

Kathak is a classical dance form originating from northern India, known for its intricate footwork, expressive facial gestures, and fast spins. It combines rhythmic movements with storytelling, often depicting ancient tales from Indian mythology. Dancers wear ankle bells (ghungroos) to accentuate their steps, coordinating with complex rhythmic patterns. Kathak is deeply rooted in both Hindu and Persian traditions, making it a unique blend of cultural influences. It is performed both as a solo dance and in group performances, often accompanied by classical music and poetry.
Ballet is a classical dance form known for its elegance and technical precision. Originating in the Italian Renaissance, it emphasizes formalized steps and graceful movements. Dancers often wear tutus and pointe shoes, showcasing a range of emotions and narratives through intricate choreography. Ballet combines artistry with rigorous training, making it a highly respected and influential dance style.
